It’s always good to get away from the usual routine, and this past weekend, we decided it was time to trade the familiar South Jersey scenery for some serious elevation. 

We packed up the car, loaded our backpacks with entirely too many trail snacks (a necessity, obviously), and headed out for a much-needed hiking trip. There is just something about getting out into the woods and unplugging that hits the reset button on life.

### The Ascent (and The Detour)

If there is one thing you should know about our hiking style, it’s that we are enthusiastic, but we aren't exactly professional navigators. 

**Here is a quick breakdown of how the hike actually went:**

* **Mile 1:** Pure optimism. The weather was perfect, the birds were singing, and we were making incredible time.
* **Mile 3:** We took what we _thought_ was a "scenic shortcut" to a waterfall. 
* **Mile 4:** We realized the scenic shortcut was actually just a deer path. We had to backtrack, but we did find a pretty cool overlook by accident!
* **The Summit:** Totally worth every misstep. The panoramic view at the top was breathtaking, and it made the burning in our calves completely fade away.

### Trail Highlights 

One of the best parts of the trip wasn't just the view from the top, but the little moments along the way. We managed to snap a great photo together near the ridge (the very one featured at the top of this post!), enjoyed a perfectly quiet lunch sitting on a sun-warmed boulder, and managed to avoid getting caught in any surprise summer thunderstorms.

**Our Top Tips for this Trail:**

1.  **Bring extra water:** It gets surprisingly steep near the end!
2.  **Trust the official map:** The deer paths are a trap.
3.  **Pack the good snacks:** Peanut butter pretzels were the MVP of this trip.

We are already looking forward to planning the next route. Until then, our hiking boots are taking a well-deserved rest by the front door. 

_Thanks for following along with our adventures!_
